Alleppey

Alleppey, also known as Alappuzha, is often called the “Venice of the East” for its enchanting backwaters and houseboat cruises. Famous for its network of canals, lagoons, and lakes, Alleppey offers a serene escape into Kerala’s natural beauty. The annual Nehru Trophy Boat Race on Punnamada Lake is a spectacular cultural event. Marari Beach and Alappuzha Beach provide peaceful seaside experiences, while temples and churches reflect the town’s heritage. Alleppey is also renowned for Ayurvedic wellness, coir products, and traditional Kerala cuisine, making it a perfect blend of nature, culture, and relaxation.

Alleppey, often called the “Venice of the East,” is one of Kerala’s most enchanting destinations, famous for its serene backwaters and houseboat cruises. The network of canals, lagoons, and lakes creates a tranquil setting where visitors can drift past lush paddy fields, coconut groves, and village life along the water’s edge. Alleppey is also known for its vibrant snake boat races during the Onam festival, adding excitement to its calm charm. With golden beaches, Ayurvedic wellness centers, and delicious seafood, the town offers a perfect blend of relaxation, culture, and natural beauty. As a gateway to Kerala’s backwaters, Alleppey remains a timeless symbol of the state’s unique allure.
