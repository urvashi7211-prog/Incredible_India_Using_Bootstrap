Munnar

Munnar, nestled in the Western Ghats of Kerala, is a breathtaking hill station known for its rolling tea plantations, misty valleys, and cool climate. Once a summer retreat for the British, Munnar is now a popular destination for nature lovers and honeymooners. The lush greenery, waterfalls, and wildlife sanctuaries make it a paradise for travelers. Famous attractions include Eravikulam National Park, home to the endangered Nilgiri Tahr, and scenic viewpoints like Top Station. Munnar’s tea estates, spice gardens, and tranquil atmosphere make it one of Kerala’s most enchanting destinations.

Munnar, often called the “Kashmir of South India,” is a breathtaking hill station in Kerala known for its rolling tea plantations, misty valleys, and cool climate. Nestled in the Western Ghats, it offers stunning landscapes dotted with waterfalls, lakes, and wildlife sanctuaries. The endless stretches of green tea gardens, especially around Kolukkumalai—the world’s highest tea plantation—make Munnar a paradise for nature lovers. Popular spots like Eravikulam National Park, home to the endangered Nilgiri Tahr, and scenic viewpoints such as Top Station and Echo Point add to its charm. With its blend of natural beauty, adventure, and tranquility, Munnar remains one of India’s most enchanting retreats for travelers seeking peace and scenic splendor.
